🧬 The Nitric Oxide Vasodilation Pathway

Understanding the vascular mechanics of pelvic blood flow and hydraulic tension.

How Vasodilation Works

To achieve maximal vascular rigidity and output strength, your body relies on the relaxation of the smooth muscle tissue lining arterial walls. When these smooth muscles relax, the artery diametric area expands, permitting a massive rush of localized blood volume.

This sequence is triggered by a gaseous signaling molecule known as **Nitric Oxide (NO)**. Below is the physiological pathway that translates your amino acids into expansion:

Step 1

L-Citrulline Conversion

L-Citrulline convert cleanly into L-Arginine via your kidneys, bypassing hepatic digestion completely.

Step 2

eNOS Enzyme Activation (The Pycnogenol Trigger)

The endothelial Nitric Oxide Synthase (eNOS) enzyme uses L-Arginine to produce active Nitric Oxide (NO) gas.

Step 3

cGMP Synthesis & Vasodilation

NO triggers guanylyl cyclase, converting GTP to cGMP. cGMP induces smooth muscle relaxation, generating instant pelvic arterial rigidity.
